# Free LLM v1.3 development-only rehearsal

## Decision boundary

This is pre-freeze development evidence. It is not an untouched measurement, does not change the
v1.2 integrity NO-GO, and does not authorize an experimental or public release claim.

The candidate is `free-llm-detector-alpha-2` under rule contract
`cejel-free-llm-rules-v1.1-2026-07-23`.

## Golden regression

The candidate was run locally over the same 24 frozen golden repositories. It emitted the three
previously correct findings plus the two previously missed `LLM-PRV-001` findings:

- `huggingface/chat-ui` — authenticated OpenAI-compatible REST evaluation helper and a locally
  emitted discrete result without evaluation-configuration provenance; and
- `FlowiseAI/Flowise` — an explicitly marked local Flowise evaluation request and returned per-case
  result without evaluation-configuration provenance.

The regression emitted five expected findings, no additional findings, and retained the known
`LLM-IOH-001` abstention for the unsupported cross-file generated-code execution path. Against the
six known positive golden opportunities, this is development recall `5/6` and development precision
`5/5`. Golden data was used to make the change, so these are not release metrics.

## Development corpus

Twelve additional repositories were selected from public metadata, checked against every committed
golden, retired untouched, spent untouched, reserve, replacement, and conflict-exclusion record,
then committed before any clone or scan. A validator caught two initial overlaps and they were
replaced before the corpus freeze.

All twelve exact commits and Git trees verified. Scans ran through the committed Node no-egress
wrapper. Three repositories were `assessed_with_limitations`, nine were `not_applicable`, and none
emitted a finding.

This result does not estimate precision: there were no emitted findings to adjudicate. The high
`not_applicable` rate is recorded as a coverage limitation for framework-owned and
provider-abstraction call paths. The development corpus is permanently excluded from future
untouched measurement.

### Post-scan opportunity audit

The same Codex task that authored the candidate inspected the twelve development repositories
after the scans. This was a development review, not blind labeling and not independent review.

- The three assessed repositories contained fixture-backed direct model-call surfaces, but no
  complete local source-to-defect path for any of the eight rules.
- `instructor-js`, `autolabel`, `llm`, `any-llm`, `botpress`, and `OpenMemory` contained provider
  imports or wrapper/framework call paths. Imports alone do not activate a rule, and their
  effective calls or policies were not locally resolvable within the candidate contract.
- `AgentOps` exposed most direct SDK calls in examples, which the frozen contract excludes.
- `Chainlit` and `llm-workflow-engine` exposed no supported direct integration surface in eligible
  source.
- Evaluation-like names in retrieval, classification, observability, or application code were not
  treated as LLM evaluation results without a locally linked model invocation and result-emission
  path.

The audit found no bounded detector change that could be justified from this corpus without
weakening the evidence threshold. The candidate therefore remains unchanged after the development
scan. The nine `not_applicable` results are retained as declared coverage limits, not converted
into passes or inferred findings.

## Evidence-pipeline correction

The v1.2 failure mode was replaced with a committed retention utility that:

1. requires a JSON-object compatibility record;
2. writes the retained artifact with exclusive creation and mode `0600`;
3. reads the retained bytes back;
4. rejects any byte mismatch; and
5. refuses to replace an existing destination.

The trusted workflow calls this utility after evidence-bundle assembly and before raw-artifact
upload. Its byte-preservation and fail-closed behavior are covered by the calibration test suite.

## Next gate

Before a fresh untouched cohort can be consumed:

1. inspect and label the development-only corpus for missed opportunities;
2. keep unsupported abstraction paths as disclosed abstentions unless a complete local evidence
   path can be implemented without broad name-based inference;
3. create a fresh v1.3 selection policy and disjoint 24-repository untouched cohort;
4. complete required blind and double-label coverage;
5. freeze detector, workflow, thresholds, manifests, labels, and pre-result commitment;
6. run golden and verify every downloaded artifact byte-for-byte; and
7. run untouched exactly once and compute metrics automatically.
