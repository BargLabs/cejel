# Free LLM v1.3 golden gate NO-GO

## Decision

The v1.3 detector candidate is **NO-GO** for both limited experimental and public release.
The untouched cohort was not executed and remains unspent.

The machine-readable decision is
[`results/v1.3-golden-gate-no-go.json`](../results/v1.3-golden-gate-no-go.json).

## What passed

- The public pre-result commitment was committed and timestamped before execution.
- The trusted GitHub Actions golden run completed successfully over all 24 frozen repositories.
- The detector build, workflow, cohort manifest, receipts, reports, and pre-result commitment all
  match their recorded hashes.
- The no-egress execution path passed.
- Pack-free free-core output was byte-for-byte compatible with the committed baseline.
- Both server-digested artifact ZIPs were retained and matched GitHub's recorded SHA-256 values.

## Why the gate stopped

The frozen golden inventory contains 65 `present` opportunities. The detector exactly matched one
of them and missed 64. It emitted four additional findings that could not be bound to a frozen
opportunity.

Two detector-visible reviews disagreed on one of those four findings. A distinct adjudication pass
resolved the disagreement. The final result was:

- detector false positives: 0;
- real present violations omitted from the pre-result inventory: 4.

Those four records cannot be backfilled after detector output. The formal golden correction ledger
therefore cannot be completed under the locked protocol, and formal release precision and recall
are not estimable from this cycle. As a diagnostic only, the corrected minimum denominator would
contain at least 69 present opportunities, of which the candidate detected five (about 7.2%).
That number is not a release metric.

## Review disclosure

The artifact-integrity audit and finding classifications were performed by AI tasks. They are not
human reviews. The unmatched-finding decision used two first passes plus a distinct AI
adjudication. No untouched detector result was created or inspected.

## Required next cycle

1. Use the golden evidence only for bounded, fixture-backed detector development.
2. Replace manual opportunity discovery with a reproducible, independently checked exhaustive
   discovery procedure.
3. Freeze a fresh golden opportunity inventory and a new public pre-result commitment.
4. Repeat the trusted golden run.
5. Run untouched exactly once only after the corrected golden evidence, closed correction ledger,
   and formal detector freeze pass.

The locked experimental threshold remains 0.65 recall and 0.80 precision. The public threshold
remains 0.80 recall and 0.90 precision.
